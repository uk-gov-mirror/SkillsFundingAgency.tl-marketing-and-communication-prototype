HOW MUCH TIME?

An industry placement lasts for a minimum of 315 hours (or 45 days). Designed to be flexible, you can deliver them as a:

  • block of days
  • series of blocks
  • 'day release', for example one or two days a week

You'll spend some time upfront finding the right student, and our industry placement toolkit can help you with that.

HOW MUCH EFFORT?

Before a student starts, you must:

  • ensure that the work environment is safe
  • have a line manager to support the student and provide an appraisal of their time on placement

You'll receive support at every stage of the process from your partner school or college, who will make sure that students are ready to be productive members of your workforce. They'll help you plan and set objectives for the industry placement.

HOW MUCH MONEY?

Apart from the financial cost of managing the placement, industry placements should cost you very little. There is no legal requirement or expectation that students will be paid, as it is part of a course. However, you can pay students if you wish.

WHAT ABOUT OTHER COSTS?

Your school or college has access to funds, and may be able to help with upfront costs, such as providing protective gear, DBS checks, specialist equipment etc.
