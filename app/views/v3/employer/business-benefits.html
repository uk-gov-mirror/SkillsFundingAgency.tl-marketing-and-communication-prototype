BUSINESS BENEFITS OF INDUSTRY PLACEMENTS

Industry placements give you early sight of the new talent coming into your sector. In addition to social benefits, they can help you with recruitment, improve innovation, and increase your organisation's productivity.

SMARTER RECRUITMENT

A solution for entry-level jobs

Industry placements are an ideal solution for entry-level skills shortages. They also help you build a pipeline of talent for junior positions or higher-level apprenticeships.

Cost-effective pipeline for new talent

Working with local schools and colleges, you can raise awareness of placement opportunities directly with young people who are deciding on their future careers.

Develop young people into your industry

Placements introduce your organisation to the young people coming into your industry, so you can develop the skills and attitude they'll need to succeed.

CORPORATE SOCIAL RESPONSIBILITY

Enhanced brand image and profile

A commitment to industry placements will raise your organisation's profile, locally and nationally, especially as T Levels gains more public exposure with your customers and clients.

More equality and diversity

Industry placements create opportunities for a wide range of young people, including those from traditionally marginalised or excluded groups. Diversity helps organisations understand their customers better, creates better performing teams and increases innovation and creativity.

Create shared value in your community

Traditionally, businesses have helped their communities by giving some of their profits to social causes. Industry placements move organisations beyond charity to help them create 'shared value'. They still give back, but in a way that benefits both the community and their business.

Support for your staff

Improved management and mentoring skills

Placement students create opportunities for staff to act as buddies, coaches or line managers. These roles help the business by giving existing staff management and mentorship skills, both of which have been shown to increase job satisfaction.

A fresh perspective

For smaller employers, young people can be an invaluable source of new and interesting ideas that can change the way you think about doing business.

Extra help with projects

In an environment where staff are doing multiple roles and struggling to meet difficult deadlines, an industry placement student could help you support a key piece of work that you haven't had the time or resources to complete.
