Are these courses relevant to you?

If your organisation - or a team or department within it - works in any of these areas, you can look for local schools and colleges and start discussions about how to work with their students in the future.

Even if none of these courses are relevant, employers can still get involved in offering industry placements now. There are pilot schemes in operation across the country, covering a much wider range of skill areas.

HOW MUCH TIME AND EFFORT WILL IT TAKE?

How long it lasts

Each industry placement lasts a minimum of 315 hours (around 45 days). This can be done as a single block of time or as a set number of days or hours per week.

What you need in place

Your organisation must have:

a line manager to support and mentor your placement student, helping them to understand your business

a safe environment for the student to work in

opportunities for the student to develop their technical skills within your industry

Support from schools and colleges

After you have found a school or college you'd like to work with, they will support you at every stage of the process. This includes helping you:

  • get your organisation ready for placements
  • design the placement
  • sort out the necessary paperwork, for example legal, health and safety and insurance documents you may need

Paying your placement student

It's entirely your choice whether you pay the student or not.
